Any-Butter Cookies

A healthier spin on peanut butter cookies — made with almond flour & arrowroot starch

Makes 15–16 cookies

Ingredients
1 cupcreamy peanut butter
or any nut butter of choice
2eggs
2 tsppure vanilla extract
1/4 tspsea salt
1/4–1/2 cupcoconut sugar
2 Tbsppure maple syrup
1 1/4 cupsextra-fine almond flour
✶ your sub for Rorie's
1/4 cuparrowroot starch
✶ your sub for Rorie's
1 tspbaking powder
Instructions
1

Prep Your Oven & Pans

Preheat oven to 350°F. Line 2 baking sheets with parchment paper.

2

Mix the Wet Ingredients

In a large bowl, combine the nut butter, eggs, vanilla, and salt. Stir well. Then add the coconut sugar and maple syrup. Whisk by hand or use a mixer until smooth and well blended.

💡 Use a thicker nut butter. If yours is runny, refrigerate the dough for 1 hour before shaping.
3

Add the Dry Ingredients

Add the almond flour, arrowroot starch, and baking powder. Mix until a thick batter forms — it should hold together and not be runny.

4

Shape the Cookies

Scoop 2 tablespoons of batter at a time. Roll into a small ball. Place on lined baking sheets about 1 1/2 inches apart.

5

Fork Press — The Classic Look

Press the back of a fork down onto each ball. Then press again the opposite way (rotated 90°) for the classic crisscross pattern.

6

Bake & Cool

Bake 14 minutes total. Rotate the pan at 7 minutes for even baking. Cookies should be slightly golden and puffed. Don't overbake — they firm up as they cool. Let cool on the sheet a few minutes before moving.

⏱ Rotate pan halfway through. Pull them while they still look slightly underdone — they'll firm up perfectly.
🩺

Health Breakdown

Solidly "better-for-you" as cookies go. No refined flour, no refined sugar, no butter or dairy. Almond flour + arrowroot keeps it grain-free with protein and healthy fats. Still cookies — coconut sugar and maple syrup are still sugar — but the ingredient list is as clean as it gets.

✦ What's Good

  • Grain-free & gluten-free with almond flour + arrowroot
  • No refined sugar — coconut sugar has lower glycemic index (~35 vs ~65)
  • Healthy fats from nut butter and almond flour
  • Decent protein — eggs + nut butter give ~4-5g per cookie
  • Pareve — no dairy, clean ingredient list
  • All real ingredients — no artificial sweeteners or additives

✦ Watch Out For

  • Still calorie-dense — nut butter + almond flour means ~150-180 cal per cookie
  • Sugar adds up — coconut sugar is still sugar to your body
  • Easy to overeat — healthy halo can backfire; stick to 1-2
  • Arrowroot is pure starch — nutritionally empty, but only 1/4 cup total

Recipe Notes

Nut butter thickness matters. Some brands (especially almond butter) are very thin. Use a thicker one. If yours is runny, refrigerate the dough at least an hour before shaping.

Your flour sub: 1 1/4 cups extra-fine almond flour + 1/4 cup arrowroot starch replaces Rorie's Grain-Free Flour. Cookbook's own recommended swap — and way cheaper.

Sugar flexibility: Start with 1/4–1/2 cup coconut sugar. Nut butter and maple syrup already add sweetness. Bump up next batch if needed.

Super Seed Crackers

Crunchy superfood crackers packed with 5 seeds — better than store-bought

Makes 32–48 crackers

Ingredients
2/3 cupextra-fine almond flour
✶ sub for GF oats or Rorie's
1/4 cupgolden ground flax meal
1/4 cup eachhemp seeds, sesame seeds, chia seeds, sunflower seeds, and pumpkin seeds
3/4 tspsea salt
1/2 tspgarlic powder
1 tspherbs of choice (optional)
1 1/4 cupswarm water
Instructions
1

Mix & Rest

Combine all ingredients in a bowl. Allow to rest for approximately 15 minutes for the mixture to thicken.

2

Prep Oven & Pans

Preheat oven to 325°F. Line 2 metal baking sheets with parchment paper; spray or brush lightly with oil.

3

Shape & Roll the Batter

Divide mixture evenly between prepared baking sheets. With oiled hands, shape batter into a rectangle; spray or brush the top with a bit of oil. This is necessary so the parchment won't stick in the next step.

Cover the batter rectangle with parchment paper. With a rolling pin, gently roll on top of the parchment to thin the mixture and spread to cover most of the pan surface. Remove the parchment paper; adjust the shape with oiled hands; if necessary, cover again and roll out any areas that are too thick.

4

First Bake & Cut

Remove the top piece of parchment. Bake for 20 minutes. Remove the pans from the oven; allow to cool slightly. Carefully cut the crackers into rectangles or squares. Spread them apart so they aren't touching.

5

Second & Third Bake

Bake for an additional 20 minutes; turn them over. Bake for another 20 minutes. If you want them super crispy, turn off oven and leave crackers inside until they have fully cooled.

6

Cool & Store

Allow crackers to cool completely before storing in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 1 week. Crackers can be frozen for up to 3 months; defrost at room temperature.

🩺

Health Breakdown

This is one of the healthiest things you can snack on. Packed with omega-3s, fiber, protein, and minerals from 5 different seeds plus flax. No sugar at all, no eggs, no dairy. Low glycemic, filling, and genuinely nutritious.

✦ What's Good

  • Zero sugar — savory, clean ingredients only
  • Omega-3 powerhouse — flax, hemp, chia
  • High fiber — seeds + flax keep you full
  • High protein — hemp & pumpkin seeds are loaded
  • Nut-free option if using oats instead of almond flour

✦ Watch Out For

  • Long bake time — ~60 minutes total
  • Calorie-dense per weight — seeds pack energy, but portion is small
  • Flax meal is essential — it's the binder, don't skip it

Cookie Dough Bark

A no-bake freezer treat — cookie dough base with dark chocolate & Maldon sea salt

Makes 8–10 pieces

Ingredients
1 1/2 cupsRorie's Grain-Free Flour or extra-fine almond flour
1/4 cupmelted coconut oil
1/4–1/3 cuppure maple syrup
1/2 tsppure vanilla extract
1 1/2 cups50-72% chocolate chips, divided
pinchMaldon salt, for garnish (optional)
Instructions
1

Prep

Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Set aside.

2

Make the Cookie Dough

In a large bowl, mix together flour, oil, maple syrup, and vanilla until smooth.

3

Fold in Chocolate Chips

Fold in 1/2 cup chocolate chips into the dough.

4

Spread & Quick Freeze

Place cookie dough on prepared baking sheet; spread over the baking sheet, using your hands to form a thin layer. Freeze for 5-10 minutes.

5

Melt the Chocolate

While dough chills, melt remaining chocolate chips in a double boiler or microwave.

6

Top & Set

Pour melted chocolate across the dough. Top with Maldon salt, if desired. Return to freezer for at least 1 hour.

7

Cut & Enjoy

Cut into pieces and enjoy! Store in freezer.

🩺

Health Breakdown

A treat, but a cleaner one. No eggs, no refined flour, no refined sugar. Almond flour adds protein and healthy fats. Dark chocolate (50-72%) has antioxidants. The coconut oil and maple syrup keep it simple. Portion control is key — it's calorie-dense.

✦ What's Good

  • No baking required — simple prep
  • No eggs, no refined sugar
  • Dark chocolate has antioxidants
  • Grain-free with almond flour
  • Lives in the freezer — always ready

✦ Watch Out For

  • Calorie-dense — chocolate + coconut oil + almond flour
  • Sugar from maple syrup + chocolate chips
  • Easy to overeat from the freezer — cut small pieces

Cookie Sticks

Easy chocolate chip cookie sticks — no shaping needed, just bake & cut

Makes 18–24 cookies

Ingredients
1 3/4 cupsRorie's Grain-Free Flour (see notes)
3/4 cupcoconut sugar (see notes)
1/2 tspbaking soda
1/3 tspkosher salt
1/3 cupavocado oil
1 tsppure vanilla extract
1egg
2/3 cupchocolate chips
Instructions
1

Prep Oven & Pan

Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a baking sheet or 1 (9x13-inch) pan with parchment paper.

2

Mix the Wet Ingredients

In a large bowl, combine all wet ingredients: avocado oil, vanilla extract, and egg. Mix well.

3

Add Dry & Chocolate Chips

Add flour, coconut sugar, baking soda, and salt. Stir to combine. Add chocolate chips, stirring to combine.

4

Shape the Dough

Form the dough into a long narrow log on prepared baking sheet or press into prepared 9x13-inch pan.

5

Bake & Cut

Bake for 20-22 minutes. Let cookies cool for 10 minutes; cut into sticks.

6

Store

Cookies will stay fresh at room temperature for 3 days or can be kept frozen in a freezer container for 3 months. Defrost at room temperature (or eat frozen — they don't fully freeze, which is yummm!)

🩺

Health Breakdown

A cleaner chocolate chip cookie. Uses avocado oil instead of butter, coconut sugar instead of refined sugar, and grain-free flour. Still a dessert — but made with real, pronounceable ingredients.

✦ What's Good

  • Avocado oil — healthier fat than butter
  • No refined sugar — coconut sugar
  • Grain-free flour
  • Freezer-friendly — great for meal prep
  • Easy — no shaping individual cookies

✦ Watch Out For

  • 3/4 cup coconut sugar is still significant sugar
  • Chocolate chips add more sugar
  • Uses Rorie's flour — not the cheap option

Tagalong Cookies

Healthier Girl Scout copycat — almond flour shortbread + peanut butter + dark chocolate

Makes 6–8 cookies

Ingredients
1 cupalmond meal or almond flour
3 Tbspmaple syrup
2 Tbspcoconut oil, melted and cooled
1/4 cupcreamy peanut butter
1 Tbsphoney (manuka or regular)
3/4 cupdark chocolate chips
Instructions
1

Prep

Preheat oven to 350°F and line a small baking sheet with parchment paper.

2

Make the Cookie Base

In a medium bowl, mix together the almond flour, coconut oil and maple syrup. Form into cookies by rolling about 2 spoonfuls of dough into your hand and add to baking sheet. Press down slightly on top of each cookie to create an indent for the peanut butter.

3

Bake the Base

Bake in oven for 15-18 minutes then allow the cookies to cool for a few minutes.

4

Add Peanut Butter Filling

Mix together the peanut butter and honey in a small bowl then evenly fill each indent on the cookies. Place them back on baking sheet. Add cookies to freezer for 10 minutes so the PB sets.

5

Chocolate Dip

Melt the dark chocolate in a double boiler or microwave then gently dip each cookie into the melted chocolate. Add back to baking sheet.

6

Set & Enjoy

Set in fridge or freezer for about 20 minutes to set and enjoy!

🩺

Health Breakdown

A legit treat with real ingredients. Vegan, grain-free, no refined sugar. Only 6 ingredients. The small batch (6-8 cookies) is built-in portion control. Dark chocolate + peanut butter + almond flour is a solid combo nutritionally.

✦ What's Good

  • Only 6 ingredients — all real food
  • Vegan & grain-free
  • Small batch — built-in portion control
  • Protein from peanut butter + almond flour
  • No refined sugar

✦ Watch Out For

  • ~210+ cal per cookie — they're rich
  • Multiple sugar sources — maple + honey + chocolate
  • Multi-step process — bake, freeze, dip, freeze
